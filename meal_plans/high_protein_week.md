## Monday: Curried Double Chicken Breast Noodles With Flaked Almonds

**Nutrition:** 68g protein | 756 cal | 70g carbs | 23g fat
**Time:** 10 minutes | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients
- 250g diced chicken breast
- 2 medium egg noodle nests
- 15g ginger & garlic paste
- 1-2 tbsp curry powder
- 5.5g chicken stock mix
- 25g solid creamed coconut
- 80-160g fine green beans
- 30g sultanas
- 15g flaked almonds
- Vegetable oil

### Instructions
1. Boil a full kettle. Heat a large, wide-based pan with a drizzle of vegetable oil over high heat.

2. Once hot, add the diced chicken with ginger & garlic paste and curry powder. Cook for 4-5 min until starting to brown.

3. Add medium egg noodles to a pot, cover with boiled water. Bring to boil and cook for 5-6 min until cooked with a slight bite. Drain and reserve a cup of starchy noodle water.

4. Trim and chop green beans in half.

5. Chop creamed coconut roughly. Dissolve chicken stock mix and creamed coconut in 150ml boiled water (coconut stock). Add to the pan with green beans and sultanas. Cook for 3-4 min until sauce thickens.

6. Add drained noodles and stir together. Cook for 3 min further until chicken is cooked through (no pink meat). Add a splash of noodle water if looking dry.

7. Serve and garnish with flaked almonds.

## Tuesday: Oven-Baked Lean Beef Mince & Black Bean Chilli

**Nutrition:** 53g protein | 736 cal | 78g carbs | 23g fat
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients
- Lean beef mince (approx 250g)
- 390g black beans
- 1 red onion
- 1 garlic clove
- 32g tomato paste
- 5.5g beef stock mix
- 1 tbsp ground cumin
- 1-2 tsp ground smoked paprika
- 0.5-1 tsp dried chilli flakes
- 6 plain tortillas
- 40g cheddar cheese
- 80g natural yoghurt
- 5g fresh coriander
- Vegetable oil, olive oil

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 220°C/200°C fan/gas 7. Boil half a kettle. Heat a large, hob-safe oven-proof casserole dish with vegetable oil over high heat.

2. Peel and chop red onion into wedges. Slice half finely and set aside for serving.

3. Add mince to dish with onion wedges and a pinch of salt. Break up into rough pieces with a wooden spoon. Drain and rinse black beans. Crush garlic with side of knife and discard skin.

4. Add ground cumin, chilli flakes, smoked paprika and crushed garlic. Stir it all up.

5. Add black beans, tomato paste and 200ml boiled water. Add beef stock mix and bring to boil. Cover and put in oven for 20 min.

6. Chop tortillas into quarters. Add to baking tray with olive oil. Bake for 4-5 min until warmed through and lightly golden.

7. Remove lid from chilli and return to oven uncovered for final 5 min until mince is cooked (no pink meat).

8. Serve topped with torn coriander, reserved sliced onion, dollop of natural yoghurt and grated cheddar. Serve tortilla quarters on the side.

## Wednesday: Speedy Chicken Nasi-Goreng

**Nutrition:** 48g protein | 532 cal | 62g carbs | 12g fat
**Time:** 10 minutes | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients
- 250g diced chicken breast
- 300g edamame & jasmine rice
- 300g rainbow veg mix
- 2 free-range eggs
- 15g ginger & garlic paste
- 25g honey
- 15ml tamari soy sauce
- 8ml sriracha hot chilli sauce
- 0.5 tsp dried chilli flakes
- Vegetable oil

### Instructions
1. Heat a large pan with vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add diced chicken and cook for 6 min until golden.

2. Meanwhile, heat another large pan with vegetable oil over high heat. Add rainbow veg mix and cook for 3 min until slightly softened but still crunchy. Add ginger & garlic paste and edamame & jasmine rice. Cook for 2-3 min until fragrant.

3. Combine honey, tamari, sriracha and chilli flakes in a small bowl (spicy sauce).

4. Transfer chicken to rice pan. Add spicy sauce and mix well. Cook for 3 min further until chicken is cooked through (no pink meat) and everything is piping hot.

5. Return reserved pan to medium-low heat with vegetable oil. Crack eggs into pan, cover with lid and cook for 2-3 min until done to your liking.

6. Serve chicken nasi-goreng topped with fried egg. Season with salt and pepper.

## Thursday: Korean-Style Spicy Buldak Chicken Thigh With Cheese

**Nutrition:** 46g protein | 681 cal | 77g carbs | 22g fat
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients
- 250g diced chicken thigh
- 75-150g sushi rice
- 125g mozzarella
- 1 brown onion
- 1 garlic clove
- 15g fresh root ginger
- 1 spring onion
- 80g fine green beans
- 30g gochujang paste
- 15-30ml soy sauce
- 10-20ml toasted sesame oil
- 0.5-1 tsp dried chilli flakes
- 5g toasted sesame seeds
- Vegetable oil, sugar

### Instructions
1. Rinse sushi rice under cold water for 30 secs. Add to pot with 200ml cold water and pinch of salt. Bring to boil, reduce to low, cover and cook for 15 min. Remove from heat and keep covered for 10 min. Don't peek!

2. Peel and chop brown onion into wedges, separate into petals. Peel and finely chop garlic and ginger.

3. Add onion, garlic and ginger to a large pan (off heat). Add diced chicken, gochujang paste, chilli flakes, soy sauce, pinch of sugar and vegetable oil. Mix well.

4. Put pan on high heat. Cook for 4-5 min until fragrant and chicken is browning. Reduce heat to medium-high, add 50ml cold water, cover and cook for 10-12 min until chicken is cooked through.

5. Preheat grill to medium-high. Trim and halve green beans. Heat separate pan with sesame oil, add beans and cook for 6-7 min until tender. Add sesame seeds and mix.

6. Drain mozzarella and pat dry. Tear over cooked chicken. Grill for 4-5 min until golden and melted.

7. Finely slice spring onion. Serve buldak chicken in a bowl with sesame green beans and sticky rice. Garnish with spring onion.

## Friday: Feel Good Fish 'N' Chips With Veg

**Nutrition:** 26g protein | 494 cal | 56g carbs | 21g fat
**Time:** 25 minutes | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients
- 2 x 100g basa fillets
- 300g potatoes
- 30g panko breadcrumbs
- 5g chives
- 80g Tenderstem broccoli
- 80g fine green beans
- 2 carrots
- 1 lemon
- 45ml mayonnaise (3 sachets)
- Vegetable oil, olive oil, butter

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 220°C/200°C fan/gas 7. Cut potatoes (skins on) into thin chips. Add to baking tray with vegetable oil and salt. Mix and bake for 20-25 min until crisp.

2. Finely chop chives. Combine with panko breadcrumbs and salt. Add olive oil and mix until breadcrumbs are coated (chive crumb).

3. Add basa fillets to separate baking tray and pat dry. Top evenly with chive crumb, pressing in with back of spoon. Bake for 12-15 min until golden and cooked through.

4. Combine juice of 1/4 lemon with mayonnaise. Season with salt and pepper (lemony mayo).

5. Peel and chop carrots into batons.

6. Add carrot batons to pot with boiling water. Cook for 1 min, then add green beans and Tenderstem broccoli. Cook for 3-4 min until tender with bite. Drain and return to pot with a knob of butter. Keep covered.

7. Cut remaining lemon into wedges.

8. Serve breaded fish with chips, vegetables and lemony mayo. Garnish with lemon wedge.

## Saturday: Chicken Breast Pad Thai

**Nutrition:** 43g protein | 597 cal | 78g carbs | 13g fat
**Time:** 20 minutes | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients
- 125-250g diced chicken breast
- 150g rice noodles
- 1 red pepper
- 1 free-range egg
- 1 garlic clove
- 1 spring onion
- 1 lime
- 25g roasted peanuts
- 15-30ml soy sauce
- 8ml fish sauce
- 15g tamarind paste
- 20g ketjap manis
- 1 tsp cornflour
- Vegetable oil, sugar

### Instructions
1. Heat a large pan with vegetable oil over high heat. Add diced chicken and cook for 5 min until starting to brown.

2. Deseed pepper and cut into thin strips. Add to pan and cook for 6-7 min until pepper is tender and chicken is cooked through (no pink meat).

3. Add rice noodles to pot with boiled water. Bring to boil and cook for 4-5 min until cooked with slight bite. Drain and set aside.

4. Make pad Thai sauce: Combine cornflour with 50ml boiled water. Add 2 tsp sugar, soy sauce, tamarind paste, fish sauce, ketjap manis and juice of half the lime. Mix well.

5. Chop spring onion into thirds. Finely chop peanuts. Peel and finely chop garlic. Cut remaining lime into wedges.

6. Push chicken and pepper to one side of pan. Crack egg into other side. Stir to break yolk and cook for 1-2 min until scrambled. Mix through chicken and pepper.

7. Add pad Thai sauce, garlic and spring onion. Cook for 30 secs until fragrant. Add noodles and cook for 1 min, mixing well.

8. Serve topped with chopped peanuts and lime wedge.

## Sunday: Ultimate Slow Cooked Beef Brisket & Red Wine Cottage Pie

**Nutrition:** 47g protein | 952 cal | 70g carbs | 55g fat
**Time:** 35 minutes | **Servings:** 2

### Ingredients
- 350g slow cooked beef brisket
- 1 white potato (large)
- 1 brown onion
- 1 carrot
- 1 garlic clove
- 80-160g fine green beans
- 40g cheddar cheese
- 15g crispy onions
- 32g tomato paste
- 10g red wine paste
- 5.5g beef stock mix
- 45g salted caramel dessert (Pots & Co)
- Vegetable oil, butter, milk, flour

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 220°C/200°C fan/gas 7 and boil kettle. Peel and finely dice brown onion. Dice carrot finely. Peel and finely chop garlic.

2. Heat a large pan with vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add onion and carrot, cook for 5-6 min until softening.

3. Transfer beef brisket to board (reserve juices). Shred apart using 2 forks ("pulling"). Grate cheddar finely.

4. Add garlic and tomato paste to veg, cook for 30 secs until fragrant. Add shredded beef with 1 tsp flour, cook for 2-3 min until coated. Add red wine paste, beef stock mix, beef juices and 250ml boiled water. Bring to boil then transfer to oven-proof dish.

5. Bake uncovered for 15-20 min until tender and sauce thickened. Meanwhile, peel and chop potatoes into bite-sized pieces. Boil for 12-15 min until fork-tender. Drain and steam dry.

6. Mash potatoes with butter and splash of milk. Season with salt and pepper. Add most of the crispy onions (save some for garnish) and 1/3 of the cheese. Mix well (crispy onion mash).

7. Top beef with crispy onion mash and remaining cheese. Grill for 5-7 min until browned.

8. Trim green beans, cover with boiled water and cook for 3-4 min until tender. Drain and add butter and pepper.

9. Serve cottage pie with buttery green beans. Top with remaining crispy onions. Enjoy salted caramel dessert!
